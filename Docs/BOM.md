# Bill of Materials (BOM)

## ChairlessChair-IoT
**Development of an IoT-Enabled Wearable Lower-Limb Exoskeleton for Sit-to-Stand Assistance**

---

# Overview

This document lists the primary hardware components used during the development of the ChairlessChair-IoT wearable assistive system.

The project evolved through multiple design iterations. Initial prototypes focused on validating the mechanical structure and embedded control concepts, while the final prototype introduced improved sensing, IoT connectivity, enhanced safety mechanisms, and a redesigned mechanical frame.

---

# Engineering Considerations

The total project cost includes:

- Hardware procurement
- Mechanical fabrication
- 3D printing
- Imported linear actuators
- Customs clearance
- Battery systems
- Embedded electronics
- Wiring and assembly materials

A significant portion of the final prototype cost resulted from importing industrial-grade linear actuators and associated customs and shipping charges. The transition from the initial prototype to the final system reflects iterative engineering improvements in sensing, control architecture, mechanical robustness, and user safety.
